Method

We will be building a driver drowsiness detection system using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model. The model will be developed using TensorFlow and OpenCV to detect signs of driver drowsiness from real-time webcam footage or images.

Our model will be trained on three datasets: OC oC Drowsiness - Detection, MRL Eye Dataset, and Driver Drowsiness Dataset. These datasets provide a wide range of facial and eye imagery, improving the model's ability to generalize across individuals.

OpenCV will be used for facial feature extraction such as the eye aspect ratio (EAR), and Twilio API will be integrated to send real-time alerts when drowsiness is detected.

The primary development tools will be Python, Google Colab, and TensorFlow. These platforms offer the flexibility and GPU acceleration needed for training and testing the model.

Potential pitfalls include false positives under poor lighting conditions or interference from glasses, sunglasses, and other accessories. These issues will be addressed through data augmentation techniques and the inclusion of diverse training samples.
