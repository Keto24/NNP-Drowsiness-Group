Project Check-In

Last week, we developed a **real-time drowsiness detection system outline** focusing on **computer vision and neural networks**. Our goal was to create an alert system with **85%+ accuracy** and **real-world deployment feasibility**.

This week, we are conducting a **related works search** to explore existing research and commercial solutions. This will help us **refine our approach** and **understand the state-of-the-art** in the field.

Related Works

Drowsiness Detection using CNN (Salem & Waleed, 2024)

Summary: This research achieves **90-99.86% accuracy** using **CNNs and transfer learning** with MobileNetV2 and InceptionV3. It integrates **Haar Cascade for feature extraction** and a **mobile app for real-time detection**.

Difference from Our Project: Unlike this model, our system will combine **OpenCV-based facial extraction** with **real-time alerts (buzzer/SMS)**.

Read here

Drowsiness Detection using ANN (Dov Press)

Summary: This study uses **ANNs with facial descriptors (EAR, MAR, FL, FWB)** and **EEG signals** to detect drowsiness, achieving **60-72% accuracy**.

Difference from Our Project: We **do not use EEG signals** and aim for **higher accuracy (85%+)** using **CNNs/LSTMs**.

Read here

Recent Developments in Drowsiness Detection (Review)

Summary: A comprehensive **review of physiological, behavioral, and vehicle-based methods** for drowsiness detection, highlighting **dataset biases and real-time challenges**.

Difference from Our Project: We will focus on **behavioral methods (facial features)** with **real-time notifications**.

Read here

Drowsiness AI - Commercial Product

Summary: AI-powered **commercial driver monitoring system** that detects yawning, eye closure, and plays **audible alerts**.

Difference from Our Project: Unlike **proprietary solutions**, our model is **open-source and adaptable**.

Read here

Bosch Steering-Based Drowsiness Detection

Summary: Uses **steering angle sensors** to detect **irregular driving patterns**.

Difference from Our Project: Our system **analyzes facial features** rather than **steering behavior**.
